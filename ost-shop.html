Nsibidi
Shortbread Cookies

West Africa · Igbo Heritage · Two Editions

Nsibidi is one of the oldest indigenous writing systems in West Africa — used for centuries by the Igbo and Ekpe societies to encode meaning, ceremony, and story. These shortbread cookies press that ancient script into every surface. A script that fed meaning into every gathering. Now pressed into butter at 5am on a Sunday.

🌍 The Heritage
Nsibidi predates colonial writing systems in West Africa. Each symbol pressed into these cookies carries centuries of Igbo meaning — offered at every gathering, every table.
🔬 The Science
What makes a shortbread short? High fat, low water, no gluten development. The crumb dissolves because there is nothing holding it together — and that is precisely the point.
📦 Two Editions
Midnight — dark chocolate and espresso. Ivory — classic butter. Both pressed with Nsibidi script. Both available individually or as a paired gift box.
